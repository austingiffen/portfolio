Work Experience

Summer Research Intern – Solar Materials Lab

Institution: [Your University or Lab Name]

Project: Improving the efficiency of solar cells by exploring Copper(I) thiocyanate (CuSCN) as an alternative hole transport layer (HTL) to PEDOT:PSS.

Designed and conducted experiments to analyze the conductivity of CuSCN under various environmental conditions such as exposure to hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, and vacuum. Built hardware systems using Arduino and mass flow controllers, developed LabVIEW interfaces, and wrote Python scripts to automate data analysis and visualize results.

Investigated discrepancies in hydrogen doping results compared to published literature and identified the role of electrode spacing in conductivity enhancement. Findings indicated long-term air exposure significantly increased conductivity, suggesting new directions for HTL research.
